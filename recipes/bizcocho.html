Egg-Yolk Sponge Cake

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 150g (3/4 cup) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) milk/li>
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: zest of 1 lemon or orange for added flavor

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven and Prepare Pan:

    2. Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and line the bottom of a 9-inch round cake pan with parchment paper. You can also use a non-stick baking spray.

  2. Prepare Dry Ingredients:

    3. In a bowl, sift together the flour, salt, and baking powder. Set aside.

  3. Beat Egg Yolks:

    4. In a large mixing bowl, beat the egg yolks and sugar with an electric mixer on high speed until the mixture is thick, pale, and forms a ribbon when the beaters are lifted. This should take about 5 minutes.

  4. Add Wet Ingredients:

    5. Add the milk, vegetable oil (or melted butter), and vanilla extract to the egg yolk mixture. Mix on low speed until well combined.

  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients:

    6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing on low speed until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

  6. Bake:

    7. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean.

  7. Cool:

    8. Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Run a knife around the edges of the pan to loosen the cake, then invert it onto a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Serve:

    9. Once the cake is completely cooled, you can serve it as is, or dust it with powdered sugar for a simple decoration. You can also frost it with your favorite frosting or whipped cream.

Tips

  • Ensure all your ingredients are at room temperature before starting.
  • Be careful not to overmix the batter after adding the flour to maintain the cake’s light and airy texture.
  • This sponge cake pairs well with fresh fruits, custard, or a simple glaze.
